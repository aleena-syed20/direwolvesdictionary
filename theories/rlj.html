Background

If you don't know the famous R + L = J theory by now, maybe you deserve a couple "Shames" from a certain septa. Basically, Rhaegar Targaryan and Lyanna Stark are Jon Snow's parents. Is it a little creepy that Rhaegar ran away with a 15 year old Lyanna when he was already married and had two kids? I mean, who is to really judge (I do). Although I am a supporter of N + A = J (a post for a later date), this theory is as canon as one can get.


Evidence

Now, there is a ton of evidence out there that supports R + L = J. Below are the strongest points in support of this theory.

Bed of Blood

Dreams are often used as a way for characters to reveal certain secrets and events of their past that would otherwise be remained unsaid. Ned Stark's innermost thoughts are shown to readers in this way. Ned frequently dreams of being with Lyanna in her final moments in the Tower of Joy. He would dream "dark disturbing dreams of blood..." (GoT, Eddard XV) when imprisoned for treason in King's Landing. Childbirth was a common cause of death at this time, and mothers would often bleed out. Lyanna most likely died from childbirth, but who did she give birth to? Jon, probably.

Promise me, Ned...

As Lyanna was dying, she pleaded with Ned for him to keep a promise. We as readers do not know what exactly was exchanged between the two, but it has been suggested that Lyanna told Ned to keep her son safe from Robert and those who sought to keep the Targaryan line extinct. Ned also dreams of "broken promises" (GoT, Eddard XV). This could refer to Ned allowing Jon to be sent to the wall, and now that Ned was imprisoned for treason, is in danger for being the son of a traitor to the king.

Winter (Rose) is Coming

Blue winter roses are peak symbolism in ASoIaF. Whenever these are mentioned, Lyanna is not far off. There are many examples to present, but the most intriguing in my opinion is what Dany see in the House of the Undying. In a whirlwind of visions, Dany sees "a blue flower [growing] from a chink in a wall of ice" (CoK, Daenerys IV). This presumably depicts Jon, who is growing (or blossoming) into a man while in the Night's Watch at the Wall. It would stand to reason that someone close to Lyanna would also be symbolized by a blue rose.

Why the long face?

Jon is said to take after his father, with a long face, dark brown hair, and gray eyes. Westerosi genetics strike again where families carry specific qualities that distinguish them from other houses. Well, it's useful in this case because if Jon does bear a resemblence to Ned, he must have some Stark blood.
